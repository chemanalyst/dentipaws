Dachshund Dental Care Guide

A long jaw that still causes crowding — especially at the front

📋

Common Dental Issues

Very Common

Front Tooth Crowding

Despite their elongated muzzle, Dachshunds have crowded lower front teeth (incisors and canines) that overlap and trap plaque in hard-to-reach crevices.

Very Common

Back Molar Tartar

The narrow jaw means molars at the back have limited space. Tartar accumulates rapidly on these surfaces and is extremely difficult to remove at home.

Common

Gum Disease Starting Young

Dachshunds frequently develop gingivitis by age 3–4. Without intervention this progresses to full periodontal disease with bone loss.

Moderate

Root Abscesses

Deep infection at tooth roots is more common in Dachshunds than average. These are painful and often invisible without X-rays.

⚠️

Risk Level

24%
HIGH RISK
Dachshunds defy the assumption that long-muzzled dogs have more space — their elongated but narrow jaw still creates crowding, particularly at the front. Research data consistently places them in the high-risk category despite their medium-small size.
  • Narrow elongated jaw causes front tooth crowding
  • Back molars poorly accessible for cleaning
  • Predisposition to root abscesses documented in breed
  • Small-medium size still carries elevated risk
  • Often resistant to tooth brushing — compliance is a challenge
✅

Care Routine

Daily Care

🪥

Daily brush — extra attention to front and back

Use a small angled brush to reach the back molars. Front incisors need attention too despite the longer muzzle.

🦴

Dental chew appropriate for size

Mini Dachshunds need XS size. Standard Dachshunds can use small size. Daily use is key.

💧

Water additive daily

Helps manage bacteria in the narrow back-tooth area that brushing can't fully reach.

Professional Vet Care

🏥

Dental Examination — Every 6 months, with X-rays to check root health

Full dental exam with X-rays. X-rays catch the 60% of disease that lives below the gumline. Ask specifically for a dental assessment — it's often skipped unless you request it.

🦷

Professional Scaling When Needed

Full cleaning under anesthesia removes calcified tartar that home brushing can't touch. Your vet will advise frequency based on your individual dog's condition.
