Greyhound Dental Care Guide

The highest dental risk of any breed — and most owners don't know it

📋

Common Dental Issues

Very Common

Genetically Thin Tooth Enamel

Greyhounds have a breed-specific genetic trait that causes unusually thin enamel coating on their teeth. This thin protective layer means bacteria penetrate more easily and damage teeth faster.

Very Common

Deep Gum Pockets

Many Greyhounds have naturally deeper spaces between tooth and gum — called periodontal pockets — that trap bacteria and are impossible to clean at home.

Very Common

Rapid Periodontal Progression

Once started, periodontal disease advances faster in Greyhounds than in most other breeds. A dog with mild gingivitis at one exam can have severe bone loss by the next.

Common

Silent Disease

Greyhounds are stoic dogs that rarely display pain. Owners are frequently shocked at the severity of disease found at examinations because their dog showed no obvious signs.

⚠️

Risk Level

32%
HIGH RISK
At 32%, Greyhounds have the highest documented periodontal disease rate of any breed studied — despite being large dogs. This surprises most owners, who assume large breeds are lower risk. The cause is genetic: uniquely thin enamel and a predisposition to deep gum pockets that create the perfect environment for bacteria.
  • Breed-specific genetic trait causing thin tooth enamel
  • Naturally deeper gum pockets trap bacteria inaccessibly
  • Fastest disease progression rate of commonly studied breeds
  • Stoic temperament masks pain — disease advances silently
  • Racing history means many were kenneled with limited dental care
✅

Care Routine

Daily Care

🪥

Daily brushing — most important habit for this breed

Greyhounds' thin enamel means plaque does more damage faster. Daily brushing is essential, not optional.

💧

Water additive — daily

Helps reduce bacteria that hide in deep gum pockets where brushing can't reach.

🦴

VOHC dental chew daily

Large breeds can handle larger chews. Daily mechanical cleaning is especially helpful given deep pockets.

Professional Vet Care

🏥

Dental Examination — Every 6 months — greyhounds deteriorate very quickly

Full dental exam with X-rays. X-rays catch the 60% of disease that lives below the gumline. Ask specifically for a dental assessment — it's often skipped unless you request it.

🦷

Professional Scaling When Needed

Full cleaning under anesthesia removes calcified tartar that home brushing can't touch. Your vet will advise frequency based on your individual dog's condition.
